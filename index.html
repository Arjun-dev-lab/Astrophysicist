About Me

Sethulakshmi

Astrophysicist & Cosmology Researcher

Greetings! I'm Sethulakshmi, an astrophysicist specializing in high-energy phenomena and cosmological simulations. With over 8 years of research experience across three continents, my work bridges the gap between theoretical predictions and observational data.

My current research at the International Center for Theoretical Physics focuses on black hole accretion disks and their role in galaxy evolution. Using multi-wavelength data from cutting-edge telescopes, I develop computational models to understand these extreme environments.

Beyond research, I'm passionate about making astrophysics accessible. I've created interactive data visualization tools and regularly contribute to public science communication through podcasts, planetarium shows, and my YouTube channel "Cosmic Perspectives."

Current Primary Research

My primary research focuses on high-energy astrophysical phenomena, particularly the interaction between supermassive black holes and their host galaxies. Using multi-wavelength observations combined with hydrodynamic simulations, I study how active galactic nuclei influence star formation rates and gas dynamics in galaxy clusters.

Recent work has revealed unexpected correlations between AGN feedback mechanisms and the chemical enrichment of intracluster media. This challenges existing models of galaxy evolution and opens new avenues for understanding cosmic structure formation.
